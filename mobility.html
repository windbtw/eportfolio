My Motivations

Currently a second-year engineering student in the Software Engineering track at ENSEEIHT Toulouse, I have built a profile oriented towards software development and data analysis through my academic journey and professional experiences.

All three of my choices, located in Quebec, respond to a clear goal: acquiring internationally recognized dual expertise and benefiting from studies focused on domains that stimulate and interest me.

The video game industry, present in two of my three choices, represents for me a way to develop advanced technical skills in an environment that is a source of passion and professional motivation — essential elements for my professional fulfillment. Game development deeply interests me, as demonstrated by my S7 projects at ENSEEIHT, including the development of a video game in Java with a group of students.

The dual degree program at the University of Sherbrooke would allow me to consolidate the knowledge from my initial training at ENSEEIHT and complement it with specific aspects of game development taught there. Moreover, the various partnerships with global video game industry players would give me access to internship opportunities perfectly aligned with my aspirations.

The general Computer Science dual degree at the University of Sherbrooke would allow me to develop my skills in Machine Learning, Cybersecurity, and Distributed Systems — continuing in line with the teachings I put into practice and deepened during my two internships at Searoutes.

Canada, and more specifically Quebec, with its dynamic ecosystem in the video game industry, is an ideal place. Whether through a dual degree at the University of Sherbrooke or an exchange at UQAC, my mobility project will allow me to complete my academic journey and prepare for professional integration in a field where I can thrive. Finally, the choice of Quebec is not coincidental — it is a place where I would like to live for several years, and obtaining a degree while discovering this region and its culture would be truly beneficial.
